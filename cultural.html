Dress Modestly

  • When visiting religious sites such as temples, mosques, and gurudwaras, it's important to wear modest clothing. This typically means covering your shoulders, avoiding sleeveless tops, and wearing skirts or pants that are below knee length.
  • For both men and women, modest clothing is appreciated in general public spaces, helping to show respect for local customs.

Greet with 'Namaste'

  • When meeting locals, use the traditional greeting 'Namaste' with hands pressed together and a slight bow. This gesture is widely appreciated and shows respect for the local culture.

Footwear Etiquette

  • Always remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, and certain shops. Some places may provide shoe racks or covers.

Avoid Public Displays of Affection

  • Public displays of affection, such as hugging and kissing, are generally frowned upon. A warm smile or a respectful nod is sufficient.

Be Mindful of Religious Practices

  • Respect ongoing religious practices and rituals. Avoid interrupting ceremonies, and maintain a quiet and respectful demeanor in places of worship.

Bargain Respectfully

  • Bargaining is common in local markets, but always do so respectfully and with a smile. Avoid aggressive haggling.

Use Right Hand for Giving and Receiving

  • When giving or receiving anything, use your right hand or both hands together. The left hand is considered impolite for such interactions.

Queuing and Waiting

  • In crowded places, such as train stations or markets, follow the local custom of queuing. Be patient and wait your turn.

Dispose of Waste Properly
